## Critical data caveat — read first

The bank does **not** contain individual 2022, 2023, 2024 PSLE Science papers. What it has instead is four aggregated buckets, all dated 2022:

| Bucket | Questions | Marks |
|---|---|---|
| PSLE Life Science MCQ 2022-2024 | 42 | 84 |
| PSLE Life Science OEQ 2022-2024 | 20 | 63 |
| PSLE Physical Science MCQ 2022-2024 | 42 | 84 |
| PSLE Physical science OEQ 2022-2024 | 18 | 66 |
| **Combined** | **122** | **297** |

297 marks ≈ 3 papers worth (PSLE Science is 100 marks per paper). The four buckets must be **summed** to represent the 2022-2024 window. Using any one of them in isolation distorts the distribution:

- Life Sci MCQ only → biased toward biology MCQ topics, loses all OEQ heft
- Physical Sci OEQ only → biased toward physics OEQ topics
- And so on

The 2025 PSLE Science paper IS in the bank as a full 100-mark paper.

The 2018-2021 window has all four actual yearly papers (400 marks total).

All comparisons below normalise to **marks per paper-equivalent** so the windows can be compared cleanly.

## Why the previous analysis showed the opposite

I owe an honest reckoning here. The earlier table I produced said Interactions was UP in 2022-2024. That was wrong. The cause:

- The first analysis script (`_psle-topic-marks.ts`) ran a dedup that kept one paper per year. For year 2022 it found four entries (the four aggregated buckets) and kept just one — `PSLE Life Science MCQ 2022-2024`.
- That single bucket has Interactions at 16 marks (only the MCQ portion, no OEQ at all). Compared against 2018-2021's full 66 marks (24 MCQ + 42 OEQ), Interactions looked sharply down — actually that part was correct, just for the wrong reason.
- The summary I sent then was either off-by-error or based on a different version of the bank. I don't have the earlier message verbatim — what I can say with confidence is that the **current corrected numbers, summing all four 2022-2024 buckets, show Interactions DOWN by 3.4 marks / paper from the 2018-2021 baseline**.

If you have a screenshot or copy of the earlier analysis, send it and I'll reconcile line-by-line.

## What I'd actually do with this

- **Don't drop ecology emphasis just because Interactions is down 3 m/p** — it's still the single largest topic. The shift is a slope, not a cliff.
- **Add weight to Heat, Electrical, Diversity, and Life cycles** in P5-P6 practice plans. Across both 2022-2024 and 2025 they're the consistent gainers.
- **Magnets and Water Cycle** look like reduced focus — confirm with the 2026 paper before deprioritising too aggressively.
- **OEQ vs MCQ split in Interactions** is worth noting: OEQ share fell sharply (42 → 23 marks). If true, recent papers test Interactions through shorter applied questions, not big structured-response problems.
