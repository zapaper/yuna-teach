# Top 3 combined-figure diagrams — for "These shapes terrify…" post

Source: PSLE Math 2017, 2019, 2021. All 4-5 mark Paper 2 questions.

## 01 — 2017 P2-Q18 (5 marks)

**The trick:** the figure is a rectangle bordered by 10 scalloped semicircles
(5 on top + 5 on bottom + 2 on the short sides via quarter circles). Pair
each top semicircle with the bottom one directly below it → they form full
circles. The answer = rectangle area + total of those paired circles.

**Why it's intimidating:** looks like a pillow or cloud. The kid has to count
the bumps, identify which are full circles vs quarter circles, and pair them
mentally.

## 02 — 2019 P2-Q16 (5 marks)

**The trick:** five identical semicircles drawn on/below a dashed horizontal
line (3 above, 2 below). Flip the 2 bottom semicircles up along the dashed
line — they fit exactly into 2 of the top ones, forming 2 full circles. The
shaded - unshaded subtraction collapses into a single calculation.

**Why it's intimidating:** different diameter labels (22cm, 12cm, 16cm)
make it look like 3 unrelated problems stacked together.

## 03 — 2021 P2-Q16 (5 marks)

**The trick:** **the rearrangement is already in the question.** Figure 1
shows a donut (cardboard with circle cut out). It's cut into 4 equal pieces
and rearranged in Figure 2 into a U-shape, 42cm across.

This is the ideal post visual — the "before" and "after" are side by side
in the original PSLE diagram. Use this as the example of "the shape was
already cut and rearranged for you — your job is just to add it up."

**Why it's intimidating:** kids see Figure 2 (U-shape) and panic, missing
that the perimeter = donut circumference (unchanged) + 4 straight cut lines.

## Post structure suggestion

1. **Slide 1** (terrify): All 3 above diagrams side-by-side. Caption:
   *"These shapes terrify parents and children. PSLE Math has had one
   every year for 10 years, worth 4-5 marks each."*

2. **Slide 2** (manageable): Re-draw each diagram with the shapes
   rearranged into clean circles/rectangles. Caption: *"But these
   shapes are manageable. The trick is the same every time —
   rearrange the curves so they cancel into a circle or fit into a
   rectangle."*

3. **Slide 3** (action): The 3 archetypes —
   - Pair semicircles → full circles (2017)
   - Flip bottom into top (2019)
   - Cut and rearrange (2021)

The 2021 image already has the "before / after" built in — no extra
drawing needed for that one.
