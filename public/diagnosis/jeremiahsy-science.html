1. Reversing Energy Transfer Direction

6 marks lost · 12% of total

The student traces the path of light or heat backwards, confusing the source of energy with the receiver.

EXAMPLE (MCQ) — P5 Focused: Human respiratory and circulatory systems

Question: During an exercise, sweat comes out from the pores (tiny openings) found in the human skin as shown below. Which of the following explains how this helps the body keep cool?

(1) The sweat loses heat to the air.
(2) The air gains heat from the sweat. ✗ JeremiahSy picked
(3) The body gains heat from the sweat.
(4) The sweat gains heat from the body. ✓ correct

Why JeremiahSy was tricked: Incorrectly thought the air gains heat from sweat, rather than the sweat gaining heat from the body to evaporate.

ADVICE

When a question involves heat transfer or seeing an object, always identify the SOURCE of the energy first. Draw an arrow from the hotter object to the colder object, or from the light source to the object and then to the eye. Trace the arrows on the paper before looking at the options.

What JeremiahSy missed: (a) Correct. The student's answer correctly identifies the property being investigated, which is directly related to the amount of light passing through. (b) Incorrect.
